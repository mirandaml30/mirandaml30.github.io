Personal Projects

  • Quantifying the Gender Wage Gap across Industry

    R, dplyr, ggplot2, Data Cleaning, Linear Modeling, Random Forest, Data Visualization

    Analyzed gender wage disparities across industries using regression modeling, visualization, and statistical inference on 2023 census data. Conducted exploratory analysis to assess skewness and outliers, then built a modeling pipeline using log–linear regression and a Gamma GLM with gender–industry interaction terms to estimate within-industry wage gaps while controlling for covariates such as education, experience, occupation, and hours worked. Identified the male income premium in each industry and quantified the drivers contributing most to inequality. Results revealed wide variability in gaps across sectors—from a 71.6% premium in Agriculture/Mining to just 13.3% in the Military—highlighting where inequities are most pronounced and where targeted policy or employer action may be most impactful.

  • The Effects of Abortion Restrictions on Women’s Incomes

    Python, numpy, pandas, statsmodels, Data Cleaning, Difference-in-Differences, Causal Inference, Data Visualization

    Gathered and analyzed 2019–2024 CPS microdata to estimate the causal impact of post-Dobbs abortion bans on women’s wage income using a difference-in-differences model. Built a reproducible Python workflow for large-scale data cleaning, feature engineering, fixed-effects modeling, and trend validation. Findings suggest women in states with abortion bans experienced an estimated $1,178 lower income (relative to non-ban states), highlighting potential economic effects of reproductive policy changes.

  • Customer Churn Prediction & Behavioral Insights

    R, tidyverse, caret, glmnet, Logistic Regression, EDA, Predictive Modeling, ROC Analysis, Data Visualization

    Analyzed multi-year customer transaction and engagement data for a large retail organization to identify the strongest predictors of customer churn (1% churn rate). Built a full R workflow including exploratory analysis, feature evaluation, logistic regression modeling, multicollinearity diagnostics, and model validation using ROC curves and cross-validated regularized regression. Findings revealed that annual income, total purchases last year, and referral score were the most influential predictors—respectively increasing churn likelihood by 0.78% and decreasing it by 1.22% and 0.91%. Insights informed targeted retention strategies, helping the business allocate marketing resources toward high-risk, high-value customer segments.
